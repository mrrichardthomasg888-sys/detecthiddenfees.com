How the Terms Analyzer Works

The contract terms analyzer AI breaks your contract down into its individual provisions and evaluates each one. Instead of a general risk score, it provides clause-by-clause analysis.

The analyzer examines payment terms, termination provisions, renewal clauses, liability limitations, indemnification requirements, warranty provisions, dispute resolution mechanisms, confidentiality obligations, non-compete restrictions, and assignment clauses.

For each provision, the analyzer determines whether the language is standard, favorable to you, or favorable to the other party.

The output is a clause-by-clause breakdown that you can review systematically with explanations in plain language.

Key Clauses the Analyzer Evaluates

Payment terms are evaluated for clarity, fairness, and completeness. Termination provisions are examined for balance.

Renewal clauses are flagged for automatic renewal language and price escalation provisions.

Liability limitations are evaluated for reasonableness. Indemnification provisions are analyzed for scope and balance.

Dispute resolution provisions including mandatory arbitration and venue selection are evaluated for fairness.

Using Clause Analysis in Negotiation

Clause-by-clause analysis gives you specific, actionable information about each provision. You can reference exact clauses and explain why they are concerning.

Not every clause needs to be changed. The analyzer helps you prioritize which provisions to negotiate.

Focus on clauses that create significant financial exposure or limit your legal rights.

Understanding Standard vs Unusual Terms

The analyzer distinguishes between standard language and unusual provisions by comparing against a database of thousands of similar contracts.

This comparison helps you understand which provisions are worth negotiating and which are likely non-negotiable.
