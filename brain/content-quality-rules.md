# 📝 Content Quality Rules

## Strict Content Governance for DetectHiddenFees.com

Every content page published on DetectHiddenFees.com must meet these quality standards. These rules exist to ensure every page earns its place — for humans and AI systems alike.

---

## Mandatory Content Requirements

### 1. Must Answer a Real User Question

Every page must address a specific, identifiable consumer question or need.

**✅ Good:**
- "How do I find hidden fees in my HVAC contract?"
- "What is an automatic renewal clause?"
- "AI contract review vs. lawyer review: which should I choose?"

**❌ Bad:**
- "Hidden fees information"
- "About contracts"
- "Financial tips"

**Validation:** Can you state the exact question this page answers in one sentence? If not, don't write it.

### 2. Must Provide a Direct Answer Near the Beginning

The answer to the primary question must appear within the first 150 words. Do not bury the answer behind fluff, introductions, or marketing language.

**Structure:**
```
H1: [The Question]
First paragraph: [The Answer — concise, factual, useful]
```

**❌ Wrong approach:**
> "In today's complex financial landscape, consumers face unprecedented challenges when navigating the intricacies of contractual agreements. That's why many people are turning to innovative solutions..."

**✅ Right approach:**
> "AI contract review uses machine learning to scan your contract for hidden fees, unfavorable terms, and risky clauses. It typically costs $0-50 per review (vs. $500+/hour for a lawyer) and returns results in under 5 minutes."

### 3. Must Be Original

Every page must contribute something unique. If a page can be summarized as "same as page X but with different words," it should not exist.

**Originality requirements:**
- At least 60% of the page content must be substantively different from any other page
- Examples must be unique to the page (no copy-pasted examples)
- At least one unique insight or data point per page
- The page must cover a distinct angle or subtopic, not just rephrase existing content

### 4. Must Add Unique Insights

Beyond being "not a duplicate," every page must actively add value through:

- **Original examples** — Realistic scenarios specific to the topic
- **Data points** — Statistics, research findings, or market data
- **Actionable advice** — Steps the reader can actually take
- **Industry context** — Why this matters in the real world
- **Trade-offs** — Honest discussion of pros, cons, and alternatives

### 5. Must Avoid Generic AI Wording

Content must sound like it was written by a human expert, not generated by an AI. Banned phrases and patterns:

**🚫 Banned phrasings:**
- "In today's rapidly evolving landscape..."
- "It is important to note that..."
- "In conclusion..."
- "As we have seen..."
- "Furthermore, it is worth mentioning..."
- "Studies have shown..." (without citing specific studies)
- "Many experts agree..." (without naming experts)
- "A comprehensive solution..."
- "Leveraging cutting-edge technology..."
- "In an increasingly complex world..."

**🚫 Banned structures:**
- Three-paragraph introductions before getting to the point
- Lists of generic "benefits" without substance
- Conclusion paragraphs that just restate the introduction
- Filler paragraphs that say nothing specific

**✅ Required style:**
- Direct, conversational tone
- Specific claims with evidence
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Concrete examples over abstract descriptions

### 6. Must Avoid Keyword Stuffing

Keywords should appear naturally, not forcibly.

**🚫 Examples of keyword stuffing:**
> "Our AI contract review tool is the best AI contract review software for AI contract review. Use our AI contract review platform for all your AI contract review needs."

**✅ Natural keyword usage:**
> "Our contract review tool uses AI to scan for hidden fees. The analysis covers pricing terms, cancellation clauses, and liability sections."

**Rule:** If a human reader would notice the repetition, it's keyword stuffing. If you wouldn't say it out loud to a friend, don't write it.

### 7. Must Avoid Repeating Existing Pages

Before creating any page, you must:
1. Search existing pages for overlapping topics
2. Check `knowledge/topic-map.json` for pages in the same cluster
3. Read the 3 most similar existing pages
4. Confirm the new page covers genuinely different ground

If more than 40% of the planned content overlaps with an existing page, **improve the existing page** instead of creating a new one.

### 8. Must Include Examples

Every informational page must include at least 2 concrete, realistic examples.

**Example quality requirements:**
- Specific dollar amounts, percentages, or real-world scenarios
- Named industries or situations (not "a company" but "an HVAC contractor")
- Both "before" and "after" where applicable
- Examples that a real consumer would actually encounter

**❌ Vague example:**
> "For instance, a service provider might include hidden charges in their contract."

**✅ Concrete example:**
> "An HVAC contractor quotes $8,500 for a system replacement. Buried in line 14 of the contract: a $450 'disposal fee' for removing the old unit — a service that's industry-standard to include. The AI flags this as a potential hidden fee because disposal is typically bundled in competitive quotes."

### 9. Must Include Practical Advice

Every page must tell the reader what to DO with the information.

**Actionable advice format:**
- **Immediate:** 1 thing they can do right now
- **This week:** 2-3 follow-up actions
- **Ongoing:** Long-term practice or habit

**Not actionable:** "Consumers should be aware of hidden fees."
**Actionable:** "Before signing, highlight every line item with a dollar amount. If any charge isn't clearly explained, ask the contractor to justify it in writing. If they refuse, get a second quote."

### 10. Must Explain Limitations

Every page about AI tools or analysis must honestly discuss limitations:

- What the AI CAN do
- What the AI CANNOT do
- When a human expert is still needed
- Edge cases the technology doesn't handle well
- Accuracy ranges (not "100% accurate")

**Honesty builds trust. Overpromising destroys it.**

### 11. Must Link to Related Topics

Every page must include 3-7 internal links to genuinely related content.

**Linking rules:**
- Links must be contextually relevant (not "for more information click here")
- Link text should describe what the user will find
- At least 1 link to a pillar/hub page
- At least 1 link to a deeper-dive subtopic
- At least 1 link to a practical tool or template (if applicable)

## Content Quality Checklist

Before publishing any page, verify:

### Question & Answer
- [ ] Page answers exactly one primary user question
- [ ] The answer appears in the first 150 words
- [ ] H1 reflects the actual question being answered

### Originality
- [ ] At least 60% substantively different from any existing page
- [ ] At least 2 unique examples not found on other pages
- [ ] At least 1 unique insight, data point, or perspective

### Writing Quality
- [ ] No banned AI phrases detected
- [ ] No keyword stuffing (natural reading aloud test passed)
- [ ] Grade 8-10 reading level
- [ ] No spelling or grammar errors
- [ ] Paragraphs under 150 words each

### Substance
- [ ] At least 2 concrete, realistic examples
- [ ] Practical, actionable advice included
- [ ] Limitations honestly discussed
- [ ] Sources cited where claims are made

### Structure
- [ ] Single H1, logical H2/H3 hierarchy
- [ ] 3-7 internal links to related content
- [ ] FAQ section (if informational page)
- [ ] "Last updated" date visible

### Value
- [ ] If a consumer reads this, they can DO something they couldn't before
- [ ] The page earns its existence — it's not filler
- [ ] A human expert would say "this is useful"

---

## Content That Should NOT Exist

Do not create:

1. **Thin doorway pages** — Pages that exist only to rank for a keyword
2. **Near-duplicate pages** — Slightly reworded versions of existing content
3. **Definition-only pages** — Pages that just define a term with no additional value
4. **List-without-substance pages** — "10 tips" with one sentence per tip
5. **AI-generated filler** — Content that sounds plausible but says nothing specific
6. **Keyword-variant pages** — Same content targeting slightly different keywords
7. **Empty tool pages** — Pages that promise a tool but don't deliver functionality

---

## The Ultimate Test

After writing any page, ask:

> **"If this were the only page on DetectHiddenFees.com that a consumer ever saw, would they be better off for having read it?"**

If the answer is no, the page is not good enough. Rewrite or don't publish.
