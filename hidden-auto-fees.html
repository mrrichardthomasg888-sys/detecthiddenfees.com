📊 1. Dealer Reserve / APR Markup

High RiskOn: Auto LoansAvg Cost: $500–$1,600+

What It Is

The difference between the interest rate a lender approves for a buyer and the higher rate the dealership actually charges. The dealer keeps this difference as profit — typically 1–3% above the buy rate.

Why Dealers Charge It

Dealer reserve is a primary profit center for dealership finance departments. On a $30,000 loan, a 2% markup adds $1,600+ in extra interest over five years. Dealers present the marked-up rate as the buyer's "approved rate" without disclosing the markup.

When It Is Legitimate

A modest markup (0.5–1%) may be reasonable compensation for arranging financing. Some states limit dealer reserve amounts.

When It May Be Excessive

When the markup exceeds 2%; when the dealer does not disclose the buy rate from the lender; when the dealer refuses to accept outside pre-approval.

Warning Signs

Dealers who focus on monthly payment rather than APR; refusal to disclose the buy rate; pressure to finance through the dealership rather than your bank.

Consumer Questions to Ask

"What is the buy rate from your lender?" "What APR markup are you adding?" "Will you match my pre-approved rate from my bank?"

Documents Where It Appears

Retail installment sales contract, Truth in Lending disclosure, loan agreement.

How AI Analysis Can Help

AI can compare the APR on your contract against current market rates for your credit profile, flag above-market APRs, and identify language that may conceal dealer reserve.

📄 2. Documentation Fee (Doc Fee)

Medium RiskOn: All Vehicle PurchasesAvg: $100–$800+

What It Is

A fee charged for processing the paperwork associated with a vehicle sale, including title transfer, registration, and filing of the sales contract. Doc fees vary wildly from under $100 to over $800 depending on the dealership and state.

Why Dealers Charge It

Dealers claim doc fees cover administrative costs. However, in many states with no regulation on doc fee amounts, dealers use them as pure profit.

When It Is Legitimate

When the fee is reasonable ($100–$200) and clearly disclosed before purchase. Some states regulate maximum doc fees.

When It May Be Excessive

When the doc fee exceeds $500 without justification; when the fee is charged in addition to separate administrative fees; when the fee is not disclosed until the finance office.

Warning Signs

Doc fees that are significantly higher than the state average; fees labeled "doc fee" plus separate "administrative" or "processing" fees.

Consumer Questions to Ask

"What is your documentation fee?" "Is this fee regulated by the state?" "Can you waive or reduce the doc fee?"

Documents Where It Appears

Purchase agreement, buyer's order, window sticker, financing contract.

How AI Analysis Can Help

AI can scan purchase documents to identify doc fees, compare them against state averages and legal limits, and flag excessive or multiple administrative fees.

🛡️ 3. Extended Warranty Markup

High RiskOn: Vehicle Service ContractsAvg Markup: 50–200%+

What It Is

The difference between the wholesale cost of an extended warranty (vehicle service contract) and the price charged to the consumer. Dealers can buy warranties at wholesale and sell them at significantly higher prices.

Why Dealers Charge It

Extended warranties carry some of the highest markups of any dealership product. A warranty that costs the dealer $500 may be sold for $1,500–$2,500.

When It Is Legitimate

When the warranty price is clearly disclosed and the consumer can compare prices across providers.

When It May Be Excessive

When the warranty price is not itemized; when the dealer refuses to provide the warranty terms before closing; when the markup exceeds 100% of the wholesale cost.

Warning Signs

High-pressure warranty sales in the finance office; warranties presented as non-negotiable; refusal to provide a contract with warranty pricing upfront.

Consumer Questions to Ask

"What is the price of the extended warranty?" "Can I purchase the warranty separately after the sale?" "Can I see the warranty contract before I buy?"

Documents Where It Appears

Vehicle service contract, purchase agreement, menu sheet, financing contract.

How AI Analysis Can Help

AI can scan financing documents to identify warranty charges, compare warranty pricing against third-party providers, and flag excessively high warranty costs relative to the vehicle price.

💵 4. GAP Insurance Markup

High RiskOn: Vehicle FinancingAvg Markup: 100–500%+

What It Is

Guaranteed Asset Protection (GAP) insurance covers the difference between what you owe on a vehicle and its actual cash value if totaled. Dealers frequently sell GAP at prices far above what it costs them — $300 wholesale vs. $1,000–$1,500 to the consumer.

Why Dealers Charge It

GAP is another high-margin finance office product. Consumers who finance most of the vehicle's value are often pressured into buying GAP without shopping for better prices.

When It Is Legitimate

When the GAP price is reasonable ($300–$500) and the consumer has a clear understanding of alternative sources (auto insurance companies often offer GAP for less).

When It May Be Excessive

When GAP is priced above $700; when the dealer does not disclose that GAP can be purchased from other sources or included in auto insurance.

Warning Signs

GAP presented as mandatory; GAP bundled into financing without itemized pricing; high-pressure sales about the risk of being "upside down" on the loan.

Consumer Questions to Ask

"How much is GAP insurance?" "Does my auto insurance already cover gap?" "Can I buy GAP from another provider?"

Documents Where It Appears

GAP waiver agreement, retail installment contract, financing menu.

How AI Analysis Can Help

AI can identify GAP charges in financing documents, compare pricing against market alternatives, and flag GAP costs that significantly exceed typical ranges.

🔑 5. VIN Etching / Theft Deterrent Fee

High RiskOn: All Vehicle PurchasesAvg: $100–$500+

What It Is

A fee for etching the vehicle identification number (VIN) onto windows or purchasing anti-theft window etching. The actual cost of etching tools is under $10, and the service can often be done for free by community programs.

Why Dealers Charge It

VIN etching is a high-margin add-on. The $200–$500 fee is nearly pure profit for the dealership. Many consumers don't realize they can decline this product.

When It Is Legitimate

VIN etching is rarely legitimate at the prices dealers charge. Community programs often offer free or low-cost etching.

When It May Be Excessive

Always excessive at typical dealership prices. The service costs pennies to perform.

Warning Signs

VIN etching or "theft deterrent" listed as a required charge; etching bundled into the purchase price without itemization.

Consumer Questions to Ask

"Can I decline VIN etching?" "How much does this cost and what does it include?"

Documents Where It Appears

Purchase agreement, buyer's order, window sticker addendum.

How AI Analysis Can Help

AI can scan purchase documents for VIN etching charges, flag them as high-margin add-ons, and identify whether they are listed as required or optional.

🎨 6. Paint / Fabric Protection Package

High RiskOn: All Vehicle PurchasesAvg: $300–$1,500+

What It Is

A dealer-applied paint sealant, fabric protector, or interior coating, sold at a significant markup. These products cost dealers $20–$50 and are sold to consumers for $300–$1,500+.

Why Dealers Charge It

Protection packages are among the highest-margin dealer add-ons. The products may provide minimal benefit, with many consumer advocates rating them as poor value.

When It Is Legitimate

Rarely. The same aftermarket protection products can be purchased and applied for a fraction of the dealer price.

When It May Be Excessive

Almost always excessive at dealer pricing. The markup is typically 500–1,000%+ over cost.

Warning Signs

"Paint protection" or "fabric guard" listed on the window sticker or purchase agreement without your request; presented as "already applied" and non-negotiable.

Consumer Questions to Ask

"I didn't ask for this — can you remove the charge?" "What is the warranty on this protection product?"

Documents Where It Appears

Purchase agreement, window sticker addendum, buyer's order.

How AI Analysis Can Help

AI can scan purchase documents for protection package charges, calculate the markup based on typical dealer cost, and flag these as high-margin add-ons that can be declined.
