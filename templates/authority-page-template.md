# 📄 Authority Page Template

## AEO/GEO-Optimized Content Template for DetectHiddenFees.com

Every new informational page must follow this template structure. This template is designed for both human readability and AI answer engine extraction.

---

## Section Specifications

### 1. TITLE (H1) — Based on a Real Question

The H1 must be either:
- A direct question a consumer would ask, OR
- A clear statement of what the page answers

**Format options:**
- "How to [Achieve X]: A Step-by-Step Guide"
- "What Is [X]? Understanding [Topic] in Plain English"
- "[X] vs [Y]: Which Is Right for You?"
- "Why [X Happens] and How to Protect Yourself"
- "Is [X] Worth It? An Honest Assessment"

**Requirements:**
- 50-70 characters (fits in search results)
- Contains the primary entity name
- Uses natural language, not keyword strings
- Immediately tells the reader what they'll learn

**❌ Bad:** "AI Contract Review Software Analysis Platform Tools 2026"
**✅ Good:** "Is AI Contract Review Accurate? What It Can and Can't Catch in 2026"

---

### 2. QUICK ANSWER (First 150 Words)

The first content after the H1. This is your featured snippet candidate.

**Structure:**
- 2-4 sentences answering the primary question directly
- Include key facts, numbers, or timeframes
- No fluff, no introduction, no "in this article we'll discuss"
- Written at 8th-10th grade reading level

**Template:**
```
[Direct answer to the question — 1-2 sentences stating the core fact.]

[Key context — 1 sentence explaining why this matters or how it works.]

[Specific detail — 1 sentence with numbers, timeframe, or comparison.]

[Brief caveat or limitation — 1 sentence for credibility.]
```

**Example:**
> AI contract review uses machine learning to scan agreements for hidden fees, risky clauses, and unfavorable terms — typically returning results in under 5 minutes. It costs $0-50 per review, compared to $500-$1,000+ per hour for a contract attorney. The AI flags 85-95% of common issues like overpriced line items, automatic renewal traps, and vague scope language. However, it does not provide legal advice and complex negotiations still benefit from human attorney review.

---

### 3. DETAILED EXPLANATION

Break the topic into logical H2 sections. Each H2 should:

- Be a complete thought, not a generic label
- Cover one distinct aspect of the topic
- Be 200-400 words (deep enough to be useful, not so long it's exhausting)

**H2 patterns to use:**
- "How [X] Works"
- "What [X] Can Detect"
- "Types of [X]"
- "The Cost of [X]"
- "When to Use [X]"
- "Key Features of [X]"
- "The [X] Process: Step by Step"

**Rules:**
- Every H2 section must include at least one specific fact or example
- No "lorem ipsum" H2s — if a section has nothing to say, don't include it
- Use H3s for sub-points within H2s
- Each paragraph: 2-4 sentences max

---

### 4. HOW IT WORKS

Explain the mechanism, process, or methodology clearly.

**For tool/methodology pages:**
```
1. [Step one — what happens first]
2. [Step two — what the system does]
3. [Step three — how results are delivered]
4. [Step four — what the user does with results]
```

**For concept/educational pages:**
- What causes this?
- What's the underlying mechanism?
- Why does this pattern exist?
- Who benefits from the status quo?

**Requirements:**
- Use numbered steps for processes
- Include timeframes ("takes 2-3 minutes")
- Mention technology used (OCR, ML, pattern matching, etc.)
- Be specific about what happens behind the scenes

---

### 5. REAL EXAMPLES (2-3 Minimum)

Each example must be:

- **Specific:** Real dollar amounts, real industries, real scenarios
- **Relatable:** Something a typical consumer would encounter
- **Complete:** Shows the problem AND the solution/outcome
- **Unique:** Not copy-pasted from another page

**Example template:**
```
### Example [N]: [Descriptive title]

**Scenario:** [Who, what, where — set the scene in 2-3 sentences]

**The Hidden Issue:** [What specifically was hidden or problematic]

**How It Was Found:** [What tool, method, or insight revealed the issue]

**The Outcome:** [What happened — money saved, contract renegotiated, fee removed]

**Key Takeaway:** [One sentence lesson for the reader]
```

**Requirements:**
- Dollar amounts must be realistic (research market rates)
- Names should be generic ("a homeowner in Texas" not "John Smith")
- Both common and edge-case examples are valuable
- Show what happens WITHOUT detection AND with detection

---

### 6. COMMON PROBLEMS / RED FLAGS

What goes wrong? What should consumers watch for?

**Format:** Bulleted list or short sections for each problem.

For each problem:
1. **What it is** (one sentence definition)
2. **Why it's a problem** (impact on consumer)
3. **How to spot it** (specific indicators)
4. **What to do** (actionable response)

**Example:**
> **🚩 Vague scope language**
>
> **What it is:** Contract describes work in general terms like "install HVAC system" without specifying brand, model, capacity, or what's included.
>
> **Why it's a problem:** The contractor can substitute cheaper equipment while charging the same price, or claim certain work "wasn't included" and bill extra.
>
> **How to spot it:** Look for missing specifications — no model numbers, no BTU ratings, no SEER ratings, no specific materials listed.
>
> **What to do:** Require exact model numbers, specifications, and a detailed scope of work BEFORE signing. If they won't provide it, find another contractor.

---

### 7. WHO BENEFITS

Clearly identify the target audience.

**Format:** Short sections for each audience type.

```
### [Audience Type]
- **Why they need this:** [Specific pain point]
- **How they benefit:** [Specific outcome]
- **When to use it:** [Trigger event or situation]
```

**Audience types to consider:**
- First-time [buyers/renters/patients/etc.]
- Experienced consumers who've been burned before
- Professionals reviewing documents for others
- Small business owners
- Seniors or vulnerable consumers
- Non-native English speakers navigating complex documents

---

### 8. LIMITATIONS & HONEST ASSESSMENT

**This section is not optional.** Honesty builds trust. Overselling destroys it.

**Required content:**
- What this CAN do (capabilities)
- What this CANNOT do (boundaries)
- Accuracy range (not "100% accurate" — be specific)
- When to use an alternative (human expert, different tool, different approach)
- Known edge cases or blind spots

**Format:**
```
### What [X] Can Do
- [Capability 1]
- [Capability 2]
- [Capability 3]

### What [X] Cannot Do
- [Limitation 1]
- [Limitation 2]
- [Limitation 3]

### When to Use a Human Expert Instead
- [Scenario where human judgment is essential]
- [Scenario where legal liability requires professional review]
- [Scenario the AI isn't designed for]
```

---

### 9. ACTION STEPS

Concrete steps the reader can take. This transforms information into empowerment.

**Three-tier format:**

```
### Immediate Action (Right Now)
1. [One thing they can do in the next 5 minutes]

### Short-Term Actions (This Week)
1. [Step 1]
2. [Step 2]
3. [Step 3]

### Long-Term Strategy (Ongoing)
- [Practice or habit to develop]
- [System or process to implement]
```

**Requirements:**
- Every step must be specific and completable
- No vague advice ("be more careful" → "highlight every dollar amount before signing")
- Include tools, templates, or resources when available (link to our pages)
- Each step should make sense in isolation (reader might only do one)

---

### 10. FAQ (5-10 Questions)

**Format:**
```html
<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>Question text here?</h3>
<p>Answer (40-80 words). Direct, no fluff.</p>

<h3>Another question?</h3>
<p>Answer (40-80 words).</p>
```

**Schema:**
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "Question text?",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "Concise answer."
      }
    }
  ]
}
```

**FAQ question sources:**
- Google "People Also Ask" for your target topic
- Related searches at bottom of Google results
- Questions that naturally arise from your content
- Common consumer misconceptions

**Rules:**
- Minimum 5, maximum 15 questions
- Questions must be unique across the site (no copy-paste FAQs)
- Answers should be concise enough for voice search/featured snippets
- Each FAQ should be genuinely frequently asked, not stuffed for SEO

---

### 11. RELATED TOPICS (3-7 Internal Links)

Contextual links to other pages on DetectHiddenFees.com.

**Format:**
```
## Related Topics

- [Descriptive link text] — [One sentence explaining what the reader will learn]
- [Descriptive link text] — [One sentence]
- [Descriptive link text] — [One sentence]
```

**Linking rules:**
- Minimum 1 link to a pillar/hub page
- Minimum 1 link to a deeper-dive subtopic
- Minimum 1 link to a practical tool or template
- Link text must describe the destination, not "click here"
- Links should feel natural — the reader should WANT to click

---

### 12. SOURCES / REFERENCES

Cite authoritative sources. This builds E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness).

**Format:**
```
## Sources

- [Source Name] — [Brief description of what they provided]
- [Source Name] — [Brief description]
- [Source Name] — [Brief description]
```

**Acceptable sources:**
- Government agencies (CFPB, FTC, HUD, state AG offices)
- Industry associations (NAR, NAHB, ACCA for HVAC)
- Academic research (cite the specific study)
- Reputable consumer advocacy organizations
- Original research by DetectHiddenFees (link to our reports)

**Not acceptable:**
- Other commercial blogs (unless they're the original source of data)
- Wikipedia (go to Wikipedia's sources instead)
- "Studies show" without naming the study
- No sources at all on factual claims

## Implementation Checklist

When building this template as an HTML page, verify:

### HTML Structure
- [ ] Single H1 matching the question
- [ ] H2s used for all major sections (12 sections above)
- [ ] H3s used only within H2 sections
- [ ] No skipped heading levels (H1 → H3 without H2)
- [ ] `<section>` or `<div>` wrapping major content blocks

### Schema
- [ ] Organization schema
- [ ] WebSite schema
- [ ] WebPage schema with `about` entity
- [ ] BreadcrumbList schema
- [ ] FAQPage schema (if FAQ section present)
- [ ] Article schema (if this is an article/guide)

### SEO
- [ ] Title tag: "[H1] | DetectHiddenFees" (50-65 chars)
- [ ] Meta description: Summary of the quick answer (120-158 chars)
- [ ] Canonical URL set
- [ ] OG tags: title, description, url, type, image
- [ ] Twitter Card: summary_large_image with all fields

### Design
- [ ] Dark theme (#020617 background)
- [ ] Inter font
- [ ] Glow orbs present
- [ ] Glass-morphism navigation
- [ ] Responsive at 320px
- [ ] "Last updated: [Date]" visible

### Quality (from content-quality-rules.md)
- [ ] Answers a real user question
- [ ] Direct answer in first 150 words
- [ ] At least 2 unique examples
- [ ] No banned AI phrases
- [ ] No keyword stuffing
- [ ] Practical, actionable advice included
- [ ] Limitations honestly discussed
- [ ] 3-7 internal links

---

> **This template is a floor, not a ceiling. If a page needs more sections to be genuinely useful, add them. The structure serves the content, not the other way around.**
