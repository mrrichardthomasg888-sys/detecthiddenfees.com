⚡ 1. Distribution Charge

StandardOn: Electric BillsAvg: 30–50% of total bill

What It Is

A charge for delivering electricity from transmission lines to your home or business. This covers the local poles, wires, transformers, and other infrastructure needed for last-mile delivery.

Why Companies Charge It

Distribution charges recover the cost of maintaining and upgrading local delivery infrastructure. These costs are regulated by state utility commissions and are generally legitimate.

When It Is Legitimate

When the charge reflects actual distribution costs, is approved by the state utility commission, and is itemized separately on the bill.

When It May Be Excessive

When distribution charges increase faster than the utility's actual cost of service; when profits from distribution exceed regulated return thresholds.

Warning Signs

Distribution charges that increase more than 10% year-over-year without clear justification.

Consumer Questions to Ask

"What is included in my distribution charge?" "Has this rate increased recently and why?"

Documents Where It Appears

Electric bills, utility rate schedules.

How AI Analysis Can Help

AI can scan utility bills to identify distribution charges, track changes over time, and flag increases that exceed typical regulated rate adjustments.

📡 2. Transmission Surcharge

StandardOn: Electric BillsAvg: 5–15% of total bill

What It Is

A charge for transporting electricity from power plants to local distribution systems through high-voltage transmission lines. Like distribution, this is generally a regulated pass-through cost.

Why Companies Charge It

Transmission costs are recovered from all ratepayers to maintain the regional grid infrastructure. Most residential consumers cannot choose an alternative transmission provider.

When It Is Legitimate

When the charge reflects regional transmission organization costs and is approved by regulators.

When It May Be Excessive

When transmission charges include costs unrelated to transmission infrastructure; when ratepayers are charged for new transmission projects that primarily benefit industrial customers.

Warning Signs

Transmission surcharges that vary significantly month-to-month without explanation.

Consumer Questions to Ask

"What transmission costs are included in my rate?" "How are transmission costs approved?"

Documents Where It Appears

Electric bills, utility rate disclosures.

How AI Analysis Can Help

AI can identify and track transmission charges over time, compare them against utility commission approved rates, and flag anomalies.

🌿 3. Renewable Energy Surcharge

Medium RiskOn: Electric BillsAvg: $2–$15/month

What It Is

A fee to support renewable energy mandates, energy efficiency programs, or renewable energy certificate purchases. While some surcharges support legitimate green energy programs, others include administrative costs and utility profit.

Why Companies Charge It

State renewable portfolio standards require utilities to source a percentage of electricity from renewables. The cost of compliance (or purchasing renewable energy credits) is passed to ratepayers.

When It Is Legitimate

When the surcharge directly funds verifiable renewable energy investments and is disclosed transparently on the bill.

When It May Be Excessive

When the surcharge includes administrative markups; when ratepayers are charged for RECs that the utility would purchase regardless; when the surcharge continues after programs are fully funded.

Warning Signs

Vague surcharge names like "environmental charge" with no program details; surcharges that don't appear to fund specific renewable programs.

Consumer Questions to Ask

"What specific program does this surcharge fund?" "How much of the surcharge goes to administration vs. renewable energy?"

Documents Where It Appears

Electric bills, green pricing program disclosures.

How AI Analysis Can Help

AI can identify renewable surcharge line items, research the corresponding program details, and flag surcharges with inadequate transparency or above-average costs.
