Before Signing a Contract: The Complete 30-Point Pre-Signing Checklist

Signing a contract without thorough review is one of the most expensive mistakes consumers make. This 30-point checklist walks you through every critical item to check before you commit — from pricing and fees to terms, scope, liability, and post-signing obligations. Use this checklist alongside our AI contract review tool for comprehensive protection.

1. Pricing & Fee Checklist

Before signing, verify every single charge you will be obligated to pay. Hidden fees are most commonly buried in the pricing section of contracts.

☐ Total All-In Price
Why it matters: The advertised or quoted price rarely includes all mandatory fees. Always request a complete all-in price that includes every fee, surcharge, tax, and charge.
💡 Ask: "Please provide a binding quote that includes every fee I will be required to pay. I will not pay any fee not listed here."
☐ Documentation & Processing Fees
Why it matters: Documentation fees ($200-$1,000) and processing fees often far exceed actual costs. These are frequently negotiable.
💡 Ask: "Can you waive the documentation fee? What does this fee actually cover?"
☐ Administrative & Service Charges
Why it matters: Vague fees labeled "administrative," "service," or "compliance" are often pure profit. Request specific explanations for each.
💡 Ask: "What specific services does this administrative fee cover? Can you itemize it?"
☐ Late Payment & Penalty Fees
Why it matters: Late fees ($25-$50) and penalty interest rates can dramatically increase costs. Check the grace period and whether fees trigger automatically.
💡 Ask: "Is there a grace period before late fees apply? Can you waive the first late fee as a courtesy?"
☐ Escalation & Price Increase Clauses
Why it matters: Contracts with built-in price increases (3-10% annually) can cost 15-30% more over the contract term than the base price suggests.
💡 Ask: "Does this contract have any automatic price increases? If so, are they capped?"
☐ Setup, Activation & Installation Fees
Why it matters: One-time setup fees ($25-$200) are often added at signing. Many providers waive these upon request.
💡 Ask: "Can the setup fee be waived? Is there a promotion that includes free activation?"
☐ Equipment & Material Costs
Why it matters: Equipment rental fees ($5-$20/month) and material markups (10-50% above cost) are significant hidden costs.
💡 Ask: "Can I purchase my own equipment to avoid rental fees? What is the material markup percentage?"
☐ Early Termination & Cancellation Penalties
Why it matters: Termination fees ($50-$500+) can lock you into a contract even if service is poor. Some contracts have escalating penalties.
💡 Ask: "What are the exact conditions and costs for terminating this contract early?"

2. Terms & Conditions Checklist

☐ Automatic Renewal Clause
Why it matters: Many contracts auto-renew at higher rates. Some require cancellation 30-60 days before the renewal date.
☐ Notice Period Requirements
Why it matters: Contracts may require written notice within specific timeframes for cancellations, changes, or disputes.
☐ Governing Law & Venue
Why it matters: If disputes must be resolved in a distant jurisdiction, your legal costs increase dramatically.
☐ Arbitration Clause
Why it matters: Mandatory arbitration prevents class actions and limits discovery. Arbitration costs may be split between parties.
☐ Assignment & Transfer Rights
Why it matters: If the company sells or transfers the contract, new terms may apply that you did not agree to.
☐ Force Majeure Provisions
Why it matters: These clauses determine who bears the cost if unexpected events prevent contract performance.
☐ Modification & Amendment Rights
Why it matters: Some contracts allow the provider to change terms without your consent, typically with "written notice."

3. Service & Scope Checklist

☐ Scope of Work Definition
Why: Vague scope descriptions invite change orders and additional charges.
☐ Specific Exclusions
Why: Exclusions define what is NOT covered. Missing exclusions can lead to unexpected charges.
☐ Change Order Process
Why: A defined change order process with pricing requirements prevents scope creep.
☐ Response Time SLAs
Why: Service level agreements define how quickly service must be provided and what remedies exist for delays.
☐ Warranty Coverage
Why: Warranty terms define what defects are covered, for how long, and who pays for remediation.
☐ Completion Timeline
Why: Missing deadlines are common. Contracts should define completion dates and penalties for delays.

4. Liability & Risk Checklist

☐ Indemnification Clause
Why: Indemnification can expose you to unlimited liability for the other party's actions. Negotiate caps.
☐ Limitation of Liability
Why: Contracts typically cap liability at the contract value or a fixed amount. Ensure this is reasonable.
☐ Insurance Requirements
Why: Service providers should carry liability insurance. Verify coverage amounts before signing.
☐ Liquidated Damages
Why: Predetermined penalty amounts should be reasonable, not punitive.
☐ Dispute Resolution Process
Why: The process for resolving disputes - from informal negotiation to formal proceedings - should be clearly defined.

5. Post-Signing Checklist

☐ Signed Copy Retention
Why: Store the signed contract digitally and physically. You will need it to enforce terms or dispute charges.
☐ Renewal Calendar
Why: Create a calendar reminder for 30 days before any renewal date to evaluate whether to continue.
☐ Audit Rights
Why: Some contracts grant audit rights. Exercise them periodically to verify compliance with pricing terms.
☐ Invoice Comparison
Why: Compare every invoice against the signed contract to verify charges match agreed-upon terms.

6. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: When should I review a contract before signing?
A: At least 24-48 hours before. Never sign under pressure.

Q: What is the most important thing to check?
A: The total all-in price including all fees, plus auto-renewal clauses and termination penalties.

Q: Should I use AI to review contracts before signing?
A: Yes. AI contract review can identify hidden fees and risky clauses in seconds as a complement to manual review.

Q: Can I negotiate contract terms before signing?
A: Yes. Most terms are negotiable before signing. After signing, negotiation is much harder.

Q: What happens if I miss a hidden fee before signing?
A: You may be legally obligated to pay it if it was disclosed in the contract. Use our AI analysis tool to screen contracts before signing.
