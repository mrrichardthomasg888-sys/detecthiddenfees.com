How Contract Risk Assessment Works

Contract risk assessment AI evaluates your document against a comprehensive framework of risk indicators. The analysis covers pricing risk, clause risk, scope risk, payment risk, and relationship risk. Each dimension is scored and explained.

Pricing risk evaluates whether the contract contains hidden fees, unclear pricing structures, escalation clauses, or markup provisions that could increase your costs above the stated amount.

Clause risk examines legal provisions that could create obligations or liabilities beyond what you expect. Automatic renewal, mandatory arbitration, limitation of liability, and indemnification clauses are all evaluated.

Scope risk assesses whether the contract clearly defines what each party is required to do. Vague language, missing deliverables, and open-ended obligations increase scope risk.

Payment risk evaluates the timing and structure of payments. Large upfront deposits, front-loaded payment schedules, and aggressive late payment terms all increase risk.

Relationship risk considers whether the contract creates a balanced relationship between the parties or tilts heavily in one direction. Unbalanced contracts are more likely to lead to disputes.

Understanding Your Risk Score

Each contract receives an overall risk assessment along with dimension-specific scores. The overall score gives you a quick sense of whether the contract deserves careful review or is relatively standard.

A low risk score means the contract appears to use standard language with balanced terms. While no contract should be signed without review, low-risk contracts typically contain few surprises.

A medium risk score means the contract has some provisions that deserve attention. These may be negotiable or may be standard for your industry. The analysis explains which terms create the risk and what you can do about them.

A high risk score means the contract contains provisions that could create significant financial or legal exposure. These contracts should be reviewed carefully before signing, and some provisions may need to be negotiated or removed.

The dimension scores help you focus your attention on the areas that matter most for your specific situation.

Using Risk Assessment in Negotiation

A contract risk assessment is most valuable when used as a preparation tool for negotiation. Before you discuss terms with the other party, review the risk assessment findings and identify your priorities.

Not all risks are worth negotiating. Some are standard industry practice and attempting to change them may harm your relationship with the other party. Others are unacceptable and should be addressed before signing.

The risk assessment helps you distinguish between these categories. It explains which provisions are unusual, which are standard, and which could create significant exposure.

When you negotiate, focus on the high-risk findings first. These are the provisions that could cause the most harm. If the other party is unwilling to address high-risk issues, that tells you something important about how they approach their agreements.
