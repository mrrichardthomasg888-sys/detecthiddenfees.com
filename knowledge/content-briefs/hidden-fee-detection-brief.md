## 4. Short Answer (Featured Snippet Candidate)

> The most common hidden fees fall into 6 categories: banking fees (overdraft, maintenance, ATM — averaging $250/year), auto dealer fees (documentation, dealer prep, vin etching — $500-$2,000 added to car price), medical billing fees (duplicate charges, facility fees, out-of-network surprises — often 20-50% of a bill), construction/contractor fees (change order markups, materials escalation, disposal fees — 10-20% of project cost), subscription fees (auto-renewals, tier creep, forgotten services — $200-$400/year), and service contract fees (early termination, cancellation, processing fees). AI detects 85-95% of these fees by scanning documents for known patterns, comparing charges against market rates, and flagging unusual line items. The total cost to the average American household: $1,500-$3,000 per year.

## 10. Examples (Planned)

### Example 1: The Banking Fee Stack
- **Scenario:** A consumer with a checking account and savings account at a major bank
- **Hidden issue:** $15/month maintenance fee, $5/visit ATM out-of-network fees, $35/occurrence overdraft fees, $12/month "paper statement" fee
- **How detected:** AI aggregated 12 months of bank statements and categorized all fees by type
- **Outcome:** Consumer switched to a credit union with no maintenance fees, saving $324/year
- **Key takeaway:** Banks stack multiple small fees that individually seem insignificant but add up to hundreds annually

### Example 2: The Car Dealer Add-Ons
- **Scenario:** A consumer buying a $28,000 car with financing
- **Hidden issue:** A $499 "documentation fee," $799 "protection package" (VIN etching + fabric protection), $395 "dealer prep fee," and a marked-up interest rate (6.9% vs 4.5% qualified rate)
- **How detected:** AI flagged each add-on as above market standard and compared the interest rate against the consumer's credit profile
- **Outcome:** Consumer negotiated $1,298 in add-ons removed and secured their own financing at 4.5%
- **Key takeaway:** Dealer fees are the most aggressively hidden — always negotiate every line item

### Example 3: The Subscription Spiral
- **Scenario:** A consumer who signed up for a free trial of a streaming service
- **Hidden issue:** After the 30-day trial, the service auto-renewed at $14.99/month and increased to $19.99/month after 6 months with no notification
- **How detected:** AI scanned 12 months of bank statements and flagged the price increase and the fact that the user hadn't used the service in 8 months
- **Outcome:** Canceled the subscription, saving $240/year, and set calendar reminders for all future free trials
- **Key takeaway:** Auto-renewal fees are designed to be forgotten — AI tracks what humans ignore
