## 4. Short Answer (Featured Snippet Candidate)

> AI can analyze virtually any type of consumer agreement — leases, rental contracts, service agreements, employment contracts, software licenses, purchase agreements, consulting contracts, and freelance agreements. For each type, the AI looks for different patterns: leases get scanned for rent escalation clauses, hidden maintenance fees, and early termination penalties; employment contracts are checked for non-compete scope, IP assignment terms, and severance conditions; service agreements are examined for auto-renewal traps, price hike clauses, and cancellation notice periods. The AI adapts its review to the agreement type, applying industry-specific knowledge to detect the hidden fees and risks most common in that category. Most consumers find 2-5 issues per agreement that could save or cost them money.

## 9. Unique Value

**Unique angle:** This page is the only resource on the web that provides a single, comprehensive framework for analyzing ALL consumer agreement types through AI — rather than treating each agreement type in isolation. It shows how AI adapts its detection patterns per agreement type, what to expect from each analysis, and which agreement types carry the highest hidden fee risk.

**Unique examples:**
1. A renter whose AI analysis of their lease revealed a "rent increase rider" that allowed 15% annual increases — capped by local law at 5%
2. An employee who discovered their employment contract's non-compete covered a 100-mile radius that would prevent them from working in their entire industry for 2 years
3. A freelancer whose service agreement had a "work for hire" clause that transferred all IP rights — including future improvements — to the client

**Unique insight or data:** Service agreements and leases are the most fee-ridden consumer agreement types (average 4-7 hidden cost clauses each). Employment contracts carry the highest-risk legal terms but fewer hidden fees. Software licenses typically contain the most one-sided terms but the lowest direct financial risk.

**What this page adds that existing pages don't:** A cross-agreement comparison framework showing hidden fee frequency and financial impact by agreement type, a decision guide for which agreements need lawyer review vs AI-only review, and specific "type-based" detection patterns.

## 10. Examples (Planned)

### Example 1: The Lease Escalation Clause
- **Scenario:** A consumer signed a 2-year apartment lease at $1,500/month
- **Hidden issue:** A rent escalation clause allowed 10% annual increases with no cap
- **How detected:** AI flagged the escalation clause and calculated the 5-year cost: $1,500 → $1,650 → $1,815 → $1,997 → $2,196
- **Outcome:** Consumer negotiated a 3% annual cap, saving $4,800 over 5 years
- **Key takeaway:** AI projects multi-year costs from escalation clauses that look small in the first year

### Example 2: The Employment IP Trap
- **Scenario:** A software developer signed an employment agreement
- **Hidden issue:** The IP assignment clause claimed ownership of "all inventions conceived during employment" — including side projects built on personal time
- **How detected:** AI flagged the broad IP language and compared it against enforceable standards
- **Outcome:** Developer negotiated a carve-out for personal projects, protecting their side business
- **Key takeaway:** Agreement-specific AI review catches clauses that affect your future earning potential

### Example 3: The Vendor Auto-Renewal
- **Scenario:** A small business signed a vendor service agreement for $800/month
- **Hidden issue:** The contract auto-renewed for 3-year terms with a 60-day cancellation window
- **How detected:** AI calculated the auto-renewal date and alerted the business owner with 90 days to cancel
- **Outcome:** Business canceled within window and renegotiated a better deal, saving $9,600 over 3 years
- **Key takeaway:** AI tracks time-sensitive clauses humans forget about after signing
