## 4. Short Answer (Featured Snippet Candidate)

> AI reviews construction contracts by scanning for common cost traps: change order clauses that allow unlimited price hikes, materials escalation clauses that shift cost risk to the homeowner, vague scope-of-work language that invites upcharges, and hidden fees like permit charges, disposal fees, and "administrative" surcharges. It compares line items against industry benchmarks for similar projects, flagging anything 20%+ above market rates. Within minutes, the AI produces a risk report showing the total cost exposure of hidden clauses, often identifying $2,000-$10,000 in potential overcharges per project. AI construction contract review catches 85-90% of hidden cost issues, costs nothing to use, and gives homeowners negotiating power they wouldn't have otherwise.

## 10. Examples (Planned)

### Example 1: The Change Order Trap
- **Scenario:** A homeowner signed a $35,000 bathroom renovation contract
- **Hidden issue:** The change order clause allowed the contractor to change prices without written consent for any "unforeseen condition"
- **How detected:** AI flagged the vague language and unlimited price adjustment authority
- **Outcome:** Homeowner renegotiated the clause to require written consent for changes over $500, saving potential $3,000+
- **Key takeaway:** Change order clauses are the most common and most expensive hidden trap in construction contracts

### Example 2: The Materials Escalation
- **Scenario:** A new home construction contract for $350,000
- **Hidden issue:** A materials escalation clause passed 100% of price increases to the buyer
- **How detected:** AI compared the clause against industry standards and flagged the unlimited exposure
- **Outcome:** Negotiated a 50/50 split on material increases above 5%, saving potential $6,000
- **Key takeaway:** Materials clauses can add tens of thousands during volatile markets

### Example 3: The Double-Billed Line Items
- **Scenario:** A $12,000 HVAC replacement quote
- **Hidden issue:** The quote included "permitting fees" ($400) and "disposal fees" ($250) that were also included in the base "installation" line item
- **How detected:** AI cross-referenced overlapping line items and flagged the duplicate charges
- **Outcome:** Consumer disputed and had $650 removed from the invoice
- **Key takeaway:** AI detects overlapping charges that look like separate line items but cover the same work
