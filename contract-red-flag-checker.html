Contract Red Flag Checker: Find Warning Signs Before You Sign

Most contracts contain language that puts you at a disadvantage. Hidden fee provisions, automatic renewal clauses, unbalanced liability terms, and vague scope definitions are common red flags. Our AI-powered checker identifies these warning signs.

Every contract contains dozens of provisions that affect your legal and financial position. Most people sign without understanding half of what they are agreeing to. This is not because they are careless. It is because contracts are written in language that is intentionally difficult to parse. AI analysis changes this by reading every word, comparing every clause against known patterns, and flagging anything that deserves your attention. Hidden fees are the most common problem found in consumer agreements. They appear under names like processing charges, administrative adjustments, service surcharges, compliance recovery fees, and account maintenance costs. The amounts may seem small individually, but they compound over time. A $5 monthly fee that you did not know about costs $60 per year. Over a five-year contract, that is $300 for nothing. AI catches these fees regardless of what name the contract uses.

Automatic renewal clauses are surprisingly common and surprisingly expensive. A clause buried on page twelve of a fifteen-page agreement might state that your contract renews automatically for another term unless you provide written notice at least sixty days before expiration. Miss that window and you are locked in for another year. AI identifies these clauses before you sign, giving you the opportunity to negotiate their removal or set reminders for the notice period.

Liability provisions often tilt heavily in favor of the party that drafted the contract. You might agree to indemnify the other party for losses caused by their own negligence. You might waive your right to sue for damages even if they breach the agreement. These provisions are buried in dense legal paragraphs that most people skip. AI reads every word and flags unbalanced liability language.

Scope of work descriptions are another frequent source of disputes. Vague language like 'comprehensive services' or 'standard support' sounds reassuring but means nothing specific. When the other party delivers less than you expected, you have no recourse because the scope was never defined. AI identifies vague scope language and recommends specific questions to clarify exactly what is included.

Payment terms deserve careful scrutiny before signing. Large upfront deposits, front-loaded payment schedules, and aggressive late fees are common in consumer contracts. Some agreements require payments for work not yet performed, creating financial risk if the project stalls or the provider fails to deliver. AI analyzes payment structures and flags terms that leave you financially exposed.

Termination rights are often asymmetrical. The provider may be able to cancel your agreement for any reason with minimal notice, while you face penalties for ending the same agreement early. This imbalance is common in service contracts and subscription agreements. AI identifies these asymmetries and provides specific language to request for balancing termination rights.

Dispute resolution provisions control what happens when things go wrong. Mandatory arbitration clauses prevent you from suing in court. Venue selection clauses may require you to travel to the other party location to resolve disputes. These provisions are often buried in boilerplate language. AI identifies them and explains the practical impact on your ability to enforce your rights.

Force majeure clauses have become increasingly important as unexpected events disrupt business and personal agreements. These clauses determine what happens when circumstances beyond anyone control prevent performance. AI evaluates whether force majeure provisions are balanced between the parties.

Confidentiality provisions protect sensitive information shared during a business relationship. Overly broad confidentiality can prevent you from using your own knowledge and experience after the relationship ends. AI identifies confidentiality language that goes beyond what is reasonable to protect legitimate business interests.

Indemnification clauses require one party to cover the other losses under specific circumstances. These provisions can create significant financial exposure if they are too broad. AI flags indemnification language that would require you to pay for losses caused by the other party negligence.

Warranty provisions define what the provider guarantees about their products or services. Limited warranties with numerous exceptions may provide little actual protection. AI evaluates warranty language against industry standards to identify gaps in coverage.

Insurance requirements in contracts specify what coverage each party must maintain. Inadequate insurance requirements can leave you exposed if the other party causes harm or damage. AI identifies insurance requirements that may not provide sufficient protection for your situation.

Record keeping provisions determine what documentation must be maintained and for how long. These provisions affect your ability to dispute charges or defend claims years after a contract ends. AI flags record keeping requirements that are inconsistent or one-sided.

Assignment provisions determine whether a party can transfer their rights and obligations to someone else without your consent. Contracts that allow unrestricted assignment can leave you dealing with a party you never agreed to work with. AI identifies assignment language that should be restricted.

Waiver of jury trial provisions require you to give up your constitutional right to have a jury decide disputes. These provisions are common in consumer contracts but many people do not realize they are agreeing to them. AI flags these provisions for your attention.

Class action waiver provisions prevent you from joining with others who have been harmed by the same party to pursue legal action collectively. These provisions significantly limit your ability to seek recourse for widespread harm. AI identifies these waivers and explains their implications.

Forum selection clauses determine where any legal dispute must be resolved. If the clause requires disputes to be handled in a different state or city, your cost of pursuing legal action increases dramatically. AI identifies forum selection clauses that create practical barriers to justice.

Attorney fee provisions determine who pays legal costs if a dispute arises. One-sided fee provisions can make it financially devastating to enforce your rights even when you are clearly in the right. AI flags unbalanced attorney fee language for negotiation.

Time is of the essence clauses make deadlines strictly enforceable. Missing a deadline by even one day can be treated as a material breach under these provisions. AI identifies these clauses and explains their practical impact on your obligations.

Entire agreement clauses state that the written contract represents the complete agreement between the parties. These provisions can prevent you from relying on verbal promises made during negotiations. AI flags these clauses so you ensure all important promises are in writing.

Severability provisions determine whether the rest of the contract remains valid if one part is found unenforceable. Without a severability clause, a single problematic provision could invalidate the entire agreement. AI identifies whether this protective provision is included.

Waiver provisions determine whether failing to enforce a right at one point prevents you from enforcing it later. These provisions affect your ability to hold the other party accountable for ongoing violations. AI evaluates whether waiver provisions are fair and balanced.

Notice provisions specify how and where formal communications must be sent. Restrictive notice requirements can make it difficult to exercise your rights under the contract. AI identifies notice provisions that create unnecessary barriers to communication.

Governing law provisions determine which state laws will be used to interpret the contract. Being subject to the laws of a distant state can create uncertainty and increase legal costs. AI identifies governing law provisions that may put you at a disadvantage.

Subcontracting provisions determine whether the other party can use third parties to perform their obligations without your approval. Unrestricted subcontracting can affect quality and accountability. AI flags subcontracting language that reduces your control over who performs the work.

Most Common Contract Red Flags

Hidden fee language is the most common red flag. It appears in many forms: administrative charges, processing fees, service surcharges, compliance adjustments, and convenience fees. The AI identifies these regardless of what terminology the contract uses.

Automatic renewal clauses renew your contract without notice. Many include price increases that take effect without your consent. A contract that seemed reasonable at signing can become expensive when it renews at higher rates.

Unbalanced liability provisions make you responsible for risks that should be shared. Indemnification clauses that require you to cover the other party losses even when you did nothing wrong are a common red flag.

Vague scope definitions let the other party interpret their obligations broadly and yours narrowly. If a contract says you will receive comprehensive services without defining what that means, you are accepting an open-ended commitment.

One-sided termination rights let the other party end the agreement easily while making it difficult for you to do the same. Look for asymmetrical notice periods, cancellation fees, and conditions that apply to only one party.

Excessive penalty clauses include late fees that far exceed reasonable costs, early termination penalties that are disproportionate to actual damages, and interest rates that compound in ways that create significant financial exposure.

The AI red flag checker identifies all of these issues and more. It provides specific references to the problematic language so you can find and review each red flag in your original document.
